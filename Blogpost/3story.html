From Gut Feeling to Growth: Pyökylä's Journey to the Power of Data Awareness

Posted by Pansy Phuong Lam | April 18, 2022


Every startup begins with a spark—an idea driven by passion and instinct. For Pyökylä, that spark ignited in Turku, Finland, in 2019. The mission was simple: make life easier for students, exchange students, and travelers by offering affordable, flexible bike rentals.


Initially, every choice—from setting the first prices to deciding when to oil the chain—was a gut feeling, guided by the direct experience of the founders and immediate customer feedback. Pyökylä was focused on community building and sustainable mobility. But as demand surged, so did complexity.

The Awakening: When Instinct Isn't Enough


The early days of relying solely on intuition are charming, but they aren't scalable. As Pyökylä grew, the team encountered patterns they couldn't ignore:

  • Why were specific bikes constantly out?
  • Why were some maintenance issues a recurring surprise?
  • How could they plan inventory for the next wave of students?

The team quickly realized that running an efficient, sustainable operation—and honoring their commitment to the community—required more than just excellent service; it required data awareness.

They started small, tracking the essentials:

  • Customer booking trends
  • Popular rental durations and routes
  • Maintenance needs and frequency
  • Customer service questions and feedback

Suddenly, what had been "just numbers" became the fuel for smarter operations. This transition marked Pyökylä’s shift from an instinct-driven startup to an insight-driven business.

Data Didn't Replace the Heart—It Amplified It


The true power of data for a community-focused startup like Pyökylä isn't about maximizing profit—it’s about optimizing service. By letting data guide their strategy, Pyökylä transformed key aspects of their business:

  • Smarter Pricing: Data analysis helped find the sweet spot, balancing affordability for students with sustainable revenue for the company.
  • Proactive Operations: Instead of reacting to broken bikes, they used maintenance data to schedule proactive repairs, ensuring maximum availability and customer satisfaction.
  • Targeted Outreach: Marketing efforts, like their "Survive Turku" initiative, were tailored based on customer data insights.

The result wasn’t just measurable growth; it was smarter growth. Pyökylä became a registered startup with highly efficient operations, all while strengthening its role as a true community hub.

The Takeaway for Every Startup

Pyökylä’s story is a vital lesson: The power of data is not reserved for Silicon Valley giants. For any startup, the journey to data awareness is the journey from guesswork to strategy. It involves:

  • Acknowledging the Limit of Intuition: Recognizing that scaling requires structure.
  • Starting Small: Identifying 3-5 critical metrics that directly impact your mission.
  • Turning Insights into Action: Using data not for reports, but for making better, more confident decisions.

Pyökylä started on two wheels, powered by a great idea. But it achieved sustainable, efficient growth by embracing the power of data insights. This awareness allowed them to scale responsibly and continue serving the heart of the Turku community better than ever before.


"Smart pricing, proactive maintenance, targeted outreach — all unlocked when Pyökylä turned to data."
