The Story of Pyökylä: Turku’s Little Startup With a Big Heart

Posted by Pansy Phuong Lam | November 13, 2021


In 2019, in the vibrant student city of Turku, Finland, a small idea quietly began to roll. It started with a simple observation: every year, new students and travelers arrived with the same challenge — How do I move around the city easily and affordably? Buying a bike was expensive, buses weren’t always convenient, and many visitors stayed only a few months. So Pyökylä was born — not from a detailed business plan, but from listening to real needs.


Affordable Bikes, Big Impact

At first, Pyökylä was just a handful of bikes and a few motivated founders. But even then, the mission was clear: offer affordable, flexible bikes to students, tourists, and anyone wanting to explore Turku. The startup quickly realized that renting bikes wasn’t just about getting from point A to point B. It was about welcoming people to a new city, giving them freedom to explore, and creating a sense of community.

More Than Just Bike Rentals

Pyökylä was never just about bikes — it was about building connections. From day one, the team organized small events, ran social media campaigns, and launched their beloved “Survive-Turku” guide — a friendly handbook for newcomers on transportation, daily life, and local culture. For many exchange students, Pyökylä became one of their first friends in Turku. Quick help, practical tips, and a reliable bike to explore the city made all the difference in those first few weeks of settling in.

Data-Driven Growth

As more customers arrived, Pyökylä grew into a small registered startup. Behind the scenes, analytics became its compass. Every booking, rental pattern, and customer message revealed insights about demand and behavior. Using this data, Pyökylä refined its pricing, predicted busy seasons, and planned when to repair or buy new bikes. Data became the quiet partner helping Turku’s little bike startup run smarter.


But analytics alone couldn’t build Pyökylä. What truly made it special was its human touch. Students from around the world arrived with questions, concerns, and a bit of nervousness about starting life in a new place. Pyökylä responded with warmth — quick replies, flexible pickup times, and even last-minute deliveries to student housing. Soon, the team created “Survive Turku,” a friendly guide filled with tips about transportation, daily life, and how to make the most of the city. For many newcomers, Pyökylä became their first friend in Finland.


Running the startup was a project in constant motion. Each season required organizing repair schedules, managing bookings, communicating with customers, and ensuring every bike was ready to go. Deliveries had to be routed efficiently, returns coordinated smoothly, and issues solved quickly. Pyökylä learned to plan ahead, adapt fast, and operate with the discipline of a much larger company.

Sustainability at the Core

With demand growing, the team sourced new bikes, spare parts, and tools — always keeping cost efficiency and sustainability in mind. Repairing, reusing, and recycling became second nature, echoing Turku’s own green values. Each bike received a second, third, or even fourth life through careful maintenance, bringing circular economy principles to everyday practice.

Creating Community Through Cycling

As the customer base expanded, so did the community. Word of mouth spread quickly, and Pyökylä began running small social media campaigns highlighting weekend biking routes, student discounts, and seasonal offers. These efforts connected exchange students with locals, turning simple rentals into shared experiences. Events and gatherings brought people together, and soon Pyökylä wasn’t just a service — it was part of Turku’s student culture.

Making a Difference, One Bike at a Time

With every delivery, another newcomer found their freedom. With every repaired bike, the city became a little greener. With every friendly message, another student felt more at home. By the time Pyökylä had fully taken shape, it had quietly transformed the daily lives of hundreds of students and travelers. It became a bridge between cultures, a champion of sustainable mobility, and a small but meaningful contributor to Turku’s urban life.


Customers praised its service, recommended it to friends, and shared stories of exploring Finland on two wheels. The startup collected this feedback, analyzed trends, and used the insights to continually refine its pricing, marketing, and operations.


From a Small Idea to a Big Heart

Pyökylä started as a small idea. But through analytics, community spirit, thoughtful service, and smart operations, it became something much larger — a symbol of connection, sustainability, and the power of simple solutions in a growing city. And all of it happened on two wheels.

“A small idea that gave a city freedom — one bike, one student, one story at a time.”
